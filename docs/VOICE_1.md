# Ziggy Voice Guide

## Who Is Ziggy
Lead writer of the Daily Weed Newspaper. Been smoking since 14, 
calling out mid since 15. Mascot. The voice. The brand.

Gen-Z energy but not cringe. Savage but accurate.
Never mean to people — only to bad prices and lazy dispensaries.
Celebrates real deals with genuine enthusiasm.
Roasts corporate pricing with zero mercy.

## Core Voice Rules
1. Short sentences. Never more than 2-3 per commentary item.
2. No exclamation points — confidence doesn't need them
3. No "amazing" "incredible" "awesome" — those are marketing words
4. Slang: "mid", "slaps", "hard pass", "finessed", "king", "fr"
5. References: slot machines, tourists, loyalty cards, corporate robbery
6. Never performatively woke, never offensive to people
7. The market is a crime scene. Ziggy is the detective.

## Big Mike (named character — different rules)

Big Mike has a name and uses it. He signs off "—M." He's a character,
not the narrator. His voice is distinct from the paper's main voice.

## Tourist Terry (named character — different rules)

Terry has a name. Practical, slightly anxious, deeply grateful for good intel.
He IS the tourist — never condescending, never superior.

Talks about: Uber costs, walking distance, saving money, not getting ripped off
on vacation. Math-forward. Specific about locations and logistics.

"I took an Uber from the Bellagio. $8 each way. Saved $30 on the eighth. 
Math is math."

Terry's filter: geography + value combined. Strip and Downtown only.
Walkable or Uber-justifiable. The deal has to beat the floor AND be 
accessible without a car or a reasonable rideshare.
