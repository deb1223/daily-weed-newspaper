## What This Profile Is

The body-relief-daytime profile is the functional counterpart to the [[pain-relief-profile]]. It solves the same underlying problem — inflammation, muscle tension, chronic pain — but without myrcene's sedating mechanism. The consumer who needs to manage pain and stay operational (work, exercise recovery, daytime chronic condition management) cannot use the full pain-relief profile, which leans on opioid-pathway myrcene as a key component.

**The core stack:** caryophyllene >0.3% + humulene >0.1% + pinene 0.05–0.4%.

The architecture:
1. **Caryophyllene** — direct CB2 receptor agonism (GPCR binding confirmed); anti-inflammatory via NF-κB pathway suppression; works peripherally without sedating the CNS — the cleanest daytime pain mechanism in the DWN collection
2. **Humulene** — structural isomer of caryophyllene; anti-inflammatory across NF-κB, PLA2, and COX pathways; documented analgesic in Fernandes (2007); deepens the caryophyllene anti-inflammatory stack without adding sedation
3. **Pinene** — dual function here: (a) acetylcholinesterase inhibition keeps cognitive function intact despite THC; (b) bronchodilatory effect improves inhalation efficiency, which matters for an inhalation-first delivery format

**How this differs from the pain-relief profile:**

| | Pain-Relief Profile | Body-Relief-Daytime Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Primary terpenes | Caryophyllene + Myrcene + Humulene | Caryophyllene + Humulene + Pinene |
| Sedation | Yes (myrcene) | No (myrcene suppressed) |
| THC potentiation | High (myrcene-mediated) | Moderate (no myrcene boost) |
| Cognitive function | Reduced | Preserved (pinene) |
| Best use | Evening pain / sleep disruption | Daytime / work / exercise recovery |

## Terpene Targets

| Terpene | Threshold | Mechanism | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| β-Caryophyllene | **>0.3%** (required) | Direct CB2 receptor agonism; peripheral anti-inflammatory and analgesic without CNS sedation | Russo (2011), LaVigne (2021), Liktor-Busa (2021) |
| α-Humulene | **>0.1%** (required) | NF-κB, PLA2, COX pathway anti-inflammatory; analgesic; natural co-terpene with caryophyllene in Chemdawg/GSC genetics | Russo (2011), Fernandes (2007) — documented in [[humulene]] |
| α-Pinene | **0.05–0.4%** (supporting) | Acetylcholinesterase inhibition (cognitive protection); bronchodilation (inhalation efficiency); low threshold acceptable here — not required at the strict >0.2% focus-profile level | Russo (2011), Perry et al. (2000) |
| β-Myrcene | **<0.2%** (suppress) | Opioid-pathway sedation is the one mechanism this profile cannot use — daytime functionality is the constraint | Russo (2011) |
| Linalool | **<0.2%** (suppress) | GABA sedation; incompatible with daytime operational goal | Russo (2011), LaVigne (2021) |

**Pinene threshold flexibility:** This profile uses a range (0.05–0.4%) rather than a floor, because the primary goal is anti-inflammatory, not cognitive. Pinene's bronchodilatory benefit is accessible at even low levels. If a product otherwise matches the caryophyllene + humulene target but has only 0.05–0.1% pinene, it still qualifies — the cognitive-protection benefit at those levels is modest but the profile integrity is maintained.

## What Must Be Avoided

| Terpene | Why to Avoid |
|---|---|
| β-Myrcene >0.2% | This is the defining constraint of the daytime profile — sedation is the disqualifier |
| Linalool >0.2% | GABA sedation; same disqualifier |
| Terpinolene >0.5% | Can produce jitteriness and anxious energy — counterproductive for pain management state |
| Nerolidol >0.1% | Sedative sesquiterpene |

**High CBD consideration:** Unlike the pain-relief profile (where high CBD is cautioned against for reducing THC-myrcene synergy), CBD here is more compatible — caryophyllene's CB2 mechanism is not THC-dependent. A balanced THC:CBD ratio (1:1 to 2:1) can work well for the body-relief-daytime profile when the consumer needs maximum anti-inflammatory effect with minimum psychoactivity.

## Cannabinoid Recommendations

**THC (flexible — profile works across ratios):** Because caryophyllene's CB2 agonism is not THC-dependent, this profile is the most flexible across cannabinoid ratios. A consumer who wants maximum functional daytime relief with minimal cognitive effect should use lower THC (10–18%) with this terpene stack. A consumer comfortable with THC can go higher (20–25%) and get caryophyllene's peripheral relief plus THC's additional analgesic contribution. The profile is valid across this range.

**CBD (complementary):** CBD's indirect CB2 influence and adenosine potentiation add to the anti-inflammatory stack without sedation. Unlike the pain-relief profile, high CBD here is not contraindicated — 1:1 ratios are a defensible recommendation for consumers managing chronic inflammation who need to stay sharp.

**CBG (mild anti-inflammatory signal):** Preliminary evidence for CBG anti-inflammatory properties; no definitive terpene-specific interaction documented in DWN primary sources. Flagged for future tracking.

## Consumption Route

**Inhalation (preferred):** Caryophyllene and humulene are both sesquiterpenes — among the most heat-stable terpenes in cannabis, with boiling points above 200°C. They survive both vaporization (at full temperature) and even combustion better than monoterpenes. Vaporize at 190–205°C to fully activate the sesquiterpene anti-inflammatory stack. Pinene activates at lower temperatures (155°C) — it will be captured on the initial draw before temperature ramps up, preserving the cognitive function component.

**Oral / Edibles:** Caryophyllene is explicitly noted by Russo as a dietary terpene — it is abundant in black pepper, cloves, and rosemary, and has documented oral bioavailability through food pathways. This makes the body-relief-daytime profile uniquely suitable for oral delivery. Caryophyllene and humulene will both absorb orally. Pinene's oral acetylcholinesterase activity is less characterized, but caryophyllene's anti-inflammatory mechanism will be present. Edibles with caryophyllene-forward genetics (Garlic Cookies, GSC, Chemdog lineage) are a legitimate recommendation for daytime pain management when inhalation is not preferred. Onset 60–120 minutes.

**Pre-rolls / Combustion:** The sesquiterpene stability advantage applies here — caryophyllene and humulene survive combustion better than any other DWN terpene class. Pre-rolls using Garlic Cookies / Chemdawg genetics will deliver meaningful caryophyllene + humulene via combustion. Pinene will be mostly degraded, but the primary anti-inflammatory stack remains functional. This profile is the most combustion-tolerant in the DWN collection after pain-relief.

## Contraindications and Cautions

- **COA-dependent:** The difference between a daytime and an evening recommendation using the same strain genetics often comes down to a single terpene number. Without a COA confirming low myrcene, this recommendation cannot be made responsibly.
- **Driving:** Anti-inflammatory daytime profile does not eliminate psychoactivity. Do not recommend for driving or operating equipment.
- **Acute breakthrough pain:** For severe acute pain episodes, the body-relief-daytime profile may be insufficient — the opioid-pathway myrcene mechanism of the [[pain-relief-profile]] delivers faster and deeper analgesia. Daytime profile is appropriate for chronic background pain management, not severe breakthrough pain.
